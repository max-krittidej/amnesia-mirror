  \item \field{Conclusion}

    For adults with cognitive and memory disability, the barrier to earning is a
    barrier to remembering and organising the ordinary structure of a day --- and in
    Thailand this leaves the great majority of them out of work. Existing aids fail
    because they demand the very capacity they are meant to replace: something to
    remember, to carry, to set up. Our innovation is an ambient smart mirror that
    removes those demands --- always on, nothing to carry, looking like ordinary
    furniture --- and provides medication reminders, orientation, and step-by-step
    prompting at the moment and place they are needed.

    The design rests on evidence: smart-mirror cognitive support has been built and
    shown feasible, and prompting technology has randomised and systematic-review
    support for improving independence and employment-related outcomes. Our
    contribution is to unite that evidence in one affordable, privacy-respecting device
    that follows the chain deliberately \emph{from learning to earning} --- steadying
    daily independence, then carrying the same prompting into work skills. Built from
    commodity hardware, designed with its users, and aimed at a large under-served
    population, it is a practical step toward turning independence into income and
    dignity for people currently excluded from both.
