  \item \field{Conclusion}

    For adults with cognitive and memory disability, the barrier to earning is often a
    barrier to remembering and organising the day. MindMirror removes the demands that
    make existing aids fail: nothing to carry, charge, open, or remember. It is an
    always-present smart mirror that provides medication reminders, orientation, and
    step-by-step prompting where routines already happen.

    The contribution is to unite feasible smart-mirror hardware with evidence-based
    prompting in one affordable, privacy-respecting device that follows the chain from
    living to learning to earning. Built from commodity hardware, co-designed with users,
    and aimed at a large under-served population, it is a practical step toward turning
    independence into income and dignity.
