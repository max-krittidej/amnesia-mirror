  \item \field{Impact on People with Disabilities and Society}

    \textbf{Reduced daily barriers.} The mirror makes routines reliable without a
    caregiver present. Evidence from micro-prompting and daily-living prompting shows
    reduced support needs and better multistep task completion~[8]. Pilot targets:
    fewer missed medication doses and less time/assistance per routine.

    \textbf{Increased access to work and income.} Only 8\% of working-age people with
    disability in Thailand reach competitive work~[3, 4]. Because assistive technology
    and prompting systems improve employment-related outcomes for people with cognitive
    disability~[10, 11], MindMirror offers a credible route from daily independence to
    work-task competence.

    \textbf{Participation and dignity.} Because the device looks like furniture rather
    than a medical monitor, it supports autonomy without stigma. It also reclaims
    caregiver time, supporting family members' own ability to remain in paid work.

    \textbf{Pilot measures.} Missed medication doses; routine completion time; support
    minutes required; independent completion of trained work-task sequences; caregiver
    hours reclaimed per week. We are clear that these are Round-1 targets, not results
    already obtained; long-term employment retention remains a later validation need.
